Pankaj's Stats

Pankaj Arjan Advani is an Indian billiards and former professional snooker player. He is a 23-time International Billiards and Snooker Federation world champion. He has won 16 billiards world titles

  • In May 2016 he won the Asian 6-Red Snooker title.
  • He became the first player in the world to hold the world title as well as the continental title at the same time after he beat Malaysian Keen Hoh Moh 7–5.
  • Advani received an entry in to the wildcard round of the Indian Open and he beat Fang Xiongman 4–2 and Elliot Slessor 4–0

Ronnie's Stats

Ronnie Antonio O'Sullivan OBE is an English professional snooker player who is the current world champion and world number one.

  • O'Sullivan has been involved with several video games, including his own Ronnie O'Sullivan's Snooker
  • He has also written two autobiographies: his first, Ronnie: The Autobiography of Ronnie O'Sullivan, was published in 2003
  • O'Sullivan has also coauthored a health and fitness book with nutritionist Rhiannon Lambert entitled Top of Your Game: Eating for Mind and Body.

Rule and regulations

  • A foul occurs when the cue ball is pocketed into a pocket
  • On the first shot after the break, the eligible player may play a push-out. This option is useful when a correct shot on the lowest ball is not possible or difficult.
  • The ball must be pocketed »cleanly. « No other ball may be touched on the way into the pocket
  • A match consists of the best 2 out of 3 games
  • THE OPENING BREAK is not a "called shot." Any player performing a break shot in 8-Ball may continue to shoot his next shot so long as he has legally pocketed any object ball on the break
  • ALTERNATING BREAK. Winner of the lag or coin flip has the option to break. Players (teams) will alternate breaking on each subsequent game.
